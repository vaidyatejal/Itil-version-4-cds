Organizational Structure
Functional vs. Divisional Structures
How to group your teams.
"Think of building houses. Under a Functional model, you hire separate plumbers, painters, and builders who do excellent work but don't talk. So the plumber punches holes in the painter's drywall. Under a Divisional model, you give every single house its own builder, painter, and plumber; it is fast, but you pay double because you duplicate tools for every house."
Work Management
Swarming Model
Swarms of support instead of passing tickets like hot potato.
"Swarming is like a hospital ER Code Blue. When a critical patient arrives, doctors, anesthesiologists, and nurses swarm the bedside together to save the patient (Drop-in Swarm) rather than passing them sequentially between slow departments."
Metrics & Measurement
Watermelon Metrics
SLA dashboards that hide customer anger.
"A restaurant promises to serve food in under 10 minutes. The waiter delivers cold, burnt food in 8 minutes. The dashboard reports green (met SLA), but the customer leaves angry (red inside). That is a Watermelon Metric."
Sourcing & Governance
SIAM (Service Integration and Management)
Managing multiple third-party suppliers seamlessly.
"A couple getting married hires a DJ, venue, and caterer. Instead of managing all four themselves, they hire a Wedding Planner (SIAM Coordinator) to orchestrate them as a single unified system."
Automation & AI
RPA vs. AI vs. AIOps
Simple automation vs. predictive intelligence.
"In a soda factory: RPA is the mechanical arm screwing caps on bottles (repeats rules exactly); AI is the scanner checking for scratch faults; AIOps is the master brain analyzing line vibrations and temperature to schedule preemptive conveyor maintenance."
Workforce Strategy
Shift-Left Strategy
Moving help closer to the customer/first tier.
"Placing a clear diagram on hotel safes so guests reset their own code via SMS instantly, rather than waiting 30 minutes for hotel security to bring a physical master key."
